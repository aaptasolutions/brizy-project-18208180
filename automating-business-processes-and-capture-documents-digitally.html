Automating Business Processes and Capture documents digitally

Home >> Solutions >> Automating Business Processes and Capture documents digitally

Capturing documents digitally refers to the process of converting physical documents into digital format. This can be done using various methods and technologies, depending on the type of documents and the desired level of quality and convenience. Here's an overview of how you can capture documents digitally:

Scanning: The most common method involves using a scanner to create digital copies of physical documents. Scanners come in various types, including flatbed scanners, sheet-fed scanners, and handheld scanners. They capture images of the document and convert them into digital files like PDFs, images, or text files.

Smartphone Apps: There are many mobile apps designed for document scanning. These apps use the smartphone's camera to capture images of documents and then enhance and crop them for better readability. They can save the scanned documents as image files or PDFs.

Document Cameras: Document cameras, also known as visual presenters, are specialized cameras designed to capture documents, books, or 3D objects. They are often used in educational and business settings for presentations and digitizing documents.

Photography: Using a regular digital camera or smartphone camera to take high-quality pictures of documents can also be an option, although the quality might not be as consistent as with dedicated scanning methods.

Optical Character Recognition (OCR): OCR software converts scanned images of text into editable and searchable text documents. This is particularly useful for digitizing printed documents and making them easier to manage and search.

Cloud-Based Services: There are cloud-based document management services that allow you to upload, store, and organize digital documents. Many of these services offer mobile apps with built-in document scanning features.

Portable Scanners: Portable scanners are compact devices that allow you to scan documents on the go. Some are small handheld devices, while others are designed to be attached to laptops.

Automatic Document Feeders (ADFs): These are features commonly found in multifunction printers and high-end scanners. ADFs can automatically scan a stack of documents, one after another, saving time and effort.

Flatbed Scanners: These scanners have a flat, glass surface where you place the document to be scanned. They are great for scanning delicate or oversized documents.

File Formats: Depending on the purpose of the digital documents, you can save them in various formats, such as PDF, JPEG, PNG, TIFF, or even in editable formats like Word or Excel using OCR.
